Common questions
What is perimenopause?
The transition period before menopause begins
Perimenopause typically starts in the mid-40s. Estrogen levels fluctuate causing irregular periods, hot flashes, and mood changes — even while periods continue.
›
How long does it last?
Timelines vary — usually 4 to 10 years
The average is around 7 years. Menopause is confirmed after 12 consecutive months without a period.
›
What is HRT?
Hormone replacement therapy and how it helps
HRT replaces estrogen the body stops making. It can reduce hot flashes, mood swings, and bone loss. Low-dose options exist — ask your doctor.
›
Mood & mental health
How estrogen affects serotonin and emotions
Estrogen influences serotonin, dopamine, and cortisol. As levels drop, anxiety and depression are common — often mistaken for unrelated issues. You're not imagining it.
›
Cultural context & support
Navigating family, faith, and community stigma
Many communities don't openly discuss menopause. This app was built for people who need support outside of those conversations.
›
When to see a doctor?
Signs it's time to seek professional support
See a doctor if symptoms disrupt your sleep, work, or relationships — or if you experience symptoms before age 40.
